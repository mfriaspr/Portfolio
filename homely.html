About the Project

Homely is an app created to help streamline communication among college housemates, providing an efficient and intuitive organizational system. This app aims to create a “home” and “heavenly” space for all housemates so they feel comfortable in a non-hostile environment. With a collection of features like chore lists, a shared events calendar, collaborative grocery lists, and a notification system for reminders, Homely will enable users to enrich their lifestyles and communication.

The Problem

Navigating student life can be a demanding journey, filled with a vast number of assignments and exams throughout the academic year. Students typically dedicate an average of 3 hours per unit enrolled, translating to a weekly commitment of 36 hours for full-time students. Most students tend to study in familiar environments such as libraries and the comfort of their own homes.

For many, university life marks the first step into independent living, often sharing spaces with non-family members. Establishing effective systems for organization and communication within shared living spaces becomes crucial. Insufficient knowledge can create misunderstandings and conflicts among housemates, potentially fostering an unwelcoming atmosphere

The Solution

The goal is to develop an app that encourages a harmonious and welcoming environment, ensuring students feel truly at home in their shared living spaces. The app’s primary aim is to help with household management, making it effortless for housemates to stay organized and on top of common tasks. By facilitating communication, the app will cultivate healthy relationships and positive interactions among housemates.

The Design Process

Research

To best determine the preferences and needs of students regarding the app’s features, I conducted a survey involving 16 participants. The diverse group consisted of both male and female students aged 18 to 24. The survey encompassed various questions regarding household organization and communication. The following questions were asked:

  • Are you currently in college?
  • Do you currently live with other college students?
  • If so, how many students live in your household (including you)?
  • How long have you lived with these housemates?
  • What's your relationship like with your housemates?
  • How often are chores rotated?
  • How are chores kept track of? Do y'all use a chart, app wheel, whiteboard, and/ or etc.?
  • Beside cleanliness, what is another issue(s) that may arise in your household?
  • What do you currently like about your organizational and communication system for your household?
  • What would you say is your biggest issue in your household concerning organization and communication?

Survey Responses

Findings

Following a thorough analysis of the survey responses, several key findings emerged:

  1. The majority of students reported having positive relationships with their housemates, regardless of the organizational or communication systems in place.
  2. A preference for a weekly chore system over daily or monthly schedules was evident among respondents.
  3. Chores were primarily tracked using traditional methods such as whiteboards or charts, with many participants expressing difficulties in reminding housemates of their responsibilities.
  4. Common housemate issues were:
    • Noise complaints
    • Splitting utility and rent costs
    • Incomplete chores
    • Grocery surplus
    • Storage issues
    • Lack of notification regarding guests in shared living areas

Competitor Analysis

After personally using and observing the apps above, I have identified some challenges for users. After some analysis of app reviews the following issues were complied:

  • Cluttered user interface
  • Overwhelming text and visuals
  • Affordability concerns with the apps
  • Synchronization issues
  • Excessive advertising interruptions
  • Inconsistent delivery of reminders and notifications

These findings highlight critical areas for improvement in user experience and functionality, which will be instrumental in enhancing the effectiveness and appeal of our app.

Recollect

Challenges

While designing the app, I faced the challenge of balancing essential features with potential add-ons. Initially envisioning an 'all-in-one' solution for housemates, I realized the risk of overwhelming users with too many options. To address this, I prioritized features based on insights from the initial survey, focusing on prominent issues such as chores and cleanliness, as highlighted by multiple participants, over less emphasized aspects like cost splitting.

Additionally, time constraints presented another hurdle. With approximately a week and a half dedicated to the project, I managed only one iteration of the design process. Ideally, I would have conducted two more iterations to refine the app further. As a result, some features didn't receive the depth of development I aimed for, which may impact the user experience.

What I Learned

This project revealed the fundamental importance of research in informing the design process of both apps and websites. Throughout this journey, considerable emphasis was placed on synthesizing and translating the findings derived from research efforts. By analyzing user feedback and identifying pain points, I gained invaluable insights into the specific challenges faced by our target audience.

The research phase played a pivotal role in shaping the direction and focus of the app design. It served as the foundation upon which key design decisions were made, guiding me in prioritizing features and functionalities that directly addressed users' needs and concerns. By aligning my design choices with the insights gained from research, I was able to create a solution that was not only user-centered but also effectively addressed the core issues identified during the investigative phase.

Essentially, this project reaffirmed the critical role of research in making informed design decisions. It identifies an understanding of user behaviors, preferences, and pain points. Going forward, I will be integrating strong research practices to ensure the creation of user-centric experiences.

Improvements

To improve the user experience and functionality of the app, I plan to perform usability testing. This invaluable process involves gathering feedback directly from real users, providing insights into their actions, thoughts, and emotions while interacting with the app. By gaining firsthand insights into user behavior, I can identify potential pain points and areas for improvement before advancing to the coding phase.

Furthermore, recognizing the constraints imposed by time during this project, I intend to go through the design process two more times. Conducting the design process multiple times will enable me to address issues, ensuring a more refined and user-friendly final product.
